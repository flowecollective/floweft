FAQ

Common questions

What are butterfly weft extensions?

+

Butterfly weft extensions are an ultra-thin hair extension method featuring a PU (polyurethane) base with pre-cut guide holes. They lay completely flat against the scalp, are virtually undetectable, and install in 15-30 minutes using beads or a sew-in technique. They're the newest evolution beyond hand-tied and genius weft extensions, offering faster install times, less shedding, and a thinner profile.

What's the difference between standard, micro, and nano k-tip extensions?

+

K-tip extensions come in three bond sizes: Standard (1g per strand) provides full-weight bonds for medium to thick hair. Micro (0.7g per strand) offers reduced-weight bonds for finer sections, side pieces, and layers. Nano (0.5g per strand) gives ultra-light bonds for hairlines, temples, and sensitive areas. Custom bond sizing ensures a comfortable, damage-free install tailored to each client's individual hair type and density.

How long do butterfly weft extensions last?

+

With proper care, butterfly weft extensions last 6-12 months. The hair itself can be reused through multiple move-ups. The ultra-thin PU base is more durable than traditional hand-tied wefts and causes less tension on natural hair, meaning longer wear time with less maintenance.

Are butterfly weft extensions better than hand-tied extensions?

+

Butterfly weft extensions offer several advantages over hand-tied: they're thinner and lighter, install in 15-30 minutes vs. 1-2 hours, lay flatter against the scalp, and shed less due to sealed PU weft construction. Many stylists are transitioning from hand-tied to butterfly weft as the preferred method in 2026. However, hand-tied still works well for certain hair types and stylist preferences.

Can I use butterfly wefts and k-tips together?

+

Yes — this is actually the recommended approach for a full, seamless install. Butterfly wefts provide the base coverage for the crown and mid-sections, while k-tip extensions handle precision work around the hairline, face frame, and any areas where individual strand placement gives a more natural look. Micro and nano k-tips are especially popular for blending at the perimeter.
