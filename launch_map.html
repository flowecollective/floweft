01
Phase One
Supplier Sourcing & Vetting
Find and evaluate manufacturers in China/Vietnam/India who specialize in butterfly weft and k-tip extensions. This is the most important phase — your supplier is your product.
Weeks 1-2
🔍
Identify Supplier Candidates
Source 5-10 potential manufacturers through trade platforms and referrals.
Action Steps
  • Search Alibaba, 1688.com, and DHgate for "butterfly weft hair factory" and "k-tip hair extensions manufacturer"
  • Filter for Gold Suppliers with Trade Assurance and 3+ years in business
  • Check if they're a factory (OEM) vs. trading company — you want the factory
  • Ask for factory videos/photos to verify they actually manufacture
  • Join hair vendor Facebook groups and Reddit communities for real referrals
  • Attend the Canton Fair virtually if timing aligns (Spring or Fall session)
Ask every supplier: "Are you a factory or trading company?" and "Can you send a video of your production floor?" Trading companies add 15-30% markup with no quality control advantage.
📋
Define Product Specs
Lock in exact specifications for both your butterfly wefts and custom k-tips before contacting anyone.
Butterfly Weft Specs to Define
  • Hair origin (raw vs. virgin vs. Remy — raw unprocessed is premium)
  • Texture options (straight, body wave, deep wave, etc.)
  • Length range (12" - 28" is typical)
  • Weight per bundle (typically 100g-120g)
  • Weft construction: thin flat weft, hand-tied or machine — butterfly wefts are ultra-thin so specify
  • Color options: natural black (#1B) to start, plus any colors you want (blonde, highlighted, etc.)
K-Tip Specs to Define
  • Keratin bond type (Italian keratin is standard for quality)
  • Bond size — you said custom-sized, so define your exact mm dimensions
  • Strand count per pack (typically 50-200 strands)
  • Strand weight (usually 0.5g - 1g per strand)
  • Length range and textures to match your weft line
Create a one-page spec sheet with all this info. Send the SAME spec sheet to every supplier so you can compare quotes apples-to-apples.
💬
Request Quotes & MOQs
Get pricing, minimum order quantities, and lead times from your shortlist.
What to Ask Each Supplier
  • Unit price per bundle/pack at different MOQ tiers (10, 50, 100, 500 units)
  • MOQ for first order vs. reorders (negotiate lower for first order)
  • Sample cost and whether it's deducted from first bulk order
  • Production lead time (typically 7-21 days depending on customization)
  • Payment terms: T/T, PayPal, Trade Assurance — avoid Western Union
  • Can they do custom packaging and private labeling?
  • Do they offer OEM/ODM (custom specs and branding)?
Expect k-tips to have higher MOQs than wefts due to custom bond sizing. Start with your most popular lengths/colors to keep initial inventory lean. A first order of 50-100 units per SKU is reasonable for testing.

02
Phase Two
Sampling & Quality Control
Order samples from your top 2-3 suppliers and rigorously test them before committing to a bulk order. Never skip this.
Weeks 3-4
📦
Order Samples (2-3 Suppliers)
Pay for samples from your top candidates. Budget $100-300 for sample testing.
Action Steps
  • Order at least 2 samples of each product type from each supplier
  • Request samples in your most popular specs (e.g., 18" body wave #1B)
  • Ask for samples to be shipped via DHL/FedEx express (5-7 days) not sea freight
  • Pay via PayPal or Trade Assurance for buyer protection
  • Ask them to label which factory/batch the samples come from
🔬
Test & Evaluate Quality
Run real-world tests on every sample — wash, heat, wear, and compare.
Quality Tests to Run
  • Shed test: Run fingers through firmly — minimal shedding = good weft construction
  • Tangle test: Wear/hang for 24-48 hours, brush — quality hair won't mat
  • Wash test: Wash 3-5 times with sulfate-free shampoo — should maintain texture
  • Heat test: Flat iron at 350°F+ — real human hair won't melt or smell like plastic
  • Bleach test: If offering blonde, test if it lifts evenly without destroying hair
  • K-tip bond test: Test heat fusion application — bond should hold firmly without crumbling
  • K-tip size check: Measure bond dimensions with calipers against your custom specs
Document EVERYTHING with photos and video. You'll use this content later for marketing AND you'll have receipts if a supplier tries to send different quality in bulk. Film yourself doing the tests.
✅
Select Supplier & Negotiate
Choose your winner and negotiate terms for your first production run.
Negotiation Points
  • Use competing quotes as leverage ("Supplier B offered me $X per unit")
  • Negotiate payment terms: 30% deposit + 70% before shipping is standard
  • Ask for a quality guarantee clause — they replace defective units at no cost
  • Get agreement on a pre-shipment inspection (you or a 3rd party checks before it ships)
  • Confirm they'll match sample quality in bulk — get this in writing
  • Discuss exclusivity if you want custom specs they won't sell to others
Consider hiring a third-party QC inspector on Alibaba (costs ~$100-200) to visit the factory and check your bulk order before it ships. Worth every penny to avoid receiving 500 units of the wrong thing.

03
Phase Three
Brand Identity & Legal Setup
Build your brand and handle the legal/business foundation. This runs in parallel with sampling — don't wait.
Weeks 3-6
✨
Brand Identity & Naming
Create a brand name, visual identity, and positioning that stands out in the hair market.
Action Steps
  • Choose a brand name — check domain availability, IG handle, and USPTO trademark database
  • Define your brand positioning: luxury vs. affordable luxury vs. mid-range?
  • Design a logo (Canva, Fiverr, or hire a designer — $50-500 range)
  • Choose brand colors and fonts that feel premium (hair extensions = aspirational)
  • Write a brand story — who are you, why this hair, what makes it different?
  • Design custom packaging: satin bags, branded boxes, thank-you cards, care instruction cards
Your packaging IS your marketing. A beautiful unboxing experience gets posted on social media for free. Budget $2-5 per unit for packaging — it's worth it. Many Asian suppliers offer custom packaging as part of their OEM service.
📄
Business & Legal Setup
Register your business, get your tax IDs, and handle the legal foundations.
Action Steps
  • Register an LLC (your state's Secretary of State website — $50-500 depending on state)
  • Get an EIN from the IRS (free, takes 5 minutes online)
  • Open a business bank account — keep business and personal finances separate
  • Get a resale/sales tax permit from your state (needed to collect sales tax)
  • File for a trademark on your brand name (USPTO, ~$250-350 per class)
  • Get basic business insurance (product liability — important for beauty products)
An LLC protects your personal assets if something goes wrong. Don't skip this. You can file yourself online in most states — you don't need a lawyer for basic LLC formation.
💰
Pricing & Financial Model
Set your pricing strategy with real margins — account for ALL costs, not just product cost.
Cost Stack to Calculate
  • Product cost: Per-unit cost from supplier
  • Shipping/freight: Sea freight + last-mile delivery (see Phase 4)
  • Customs/duties: Import duty rate for hair extensions (typically 0-5%)
  • Packaging: Branded boxes, satin bags, cards, tissue
  • Fulfillment: Shipping materials, postage to customer, labor
  • Platform fees: Shopify ($39/mo), payment processing (2.9% + $0.30)
  • Marketing: Budget 15-25% of revenue for ads and influencers
  • Target margin: Aim for 60-70% gross margin minimum for beauty/hair
Example: If your landed cost (product + shipping + duty + packaging) is $30/bundle, price retail at $85-120 depending on positioning. Hair extensions have excellent margins if you control your supply chain.

04
Phase Four
Import, Shipping & Inventory
Get your bulk order manufactured, shipped from Asia, through customs, and into your hands. This is where most first-timers get tripped up.
Weeks 5-10
🏭
Place Bulk Production Order
Commit to your first production run with your chosen supplier.
Action Steps
  • Finalize exact SKU list: which lengths × textures × colors for both wefts and k-tips
  • Start lean — 3-5 bestselling SKUs per product type, not your full dream catalog
  • Send purchase order with exact specs, quantities, and delivery date
  • Pay deposit (typically 30%) via Trade Assurance or PayPal
  • Request production photos/videos at key stages (raw hair → processing → finished)
  • Confirm custom packaging specs are correct before they print
Start with your best-sellers only. For butterfly wefts: 16", 18", 20", 22" in #1B natural black and one popular texture. For k-tips: match those specs. You can always reorder fast — don't tie up cash in slow-moving inventory.
🚢
Freight & Shipping
Choose your shipping method and get your product from factory to your door.
Shipping Options
  • Air Express (DHL/FedEx/UPS): 5-10 days, $5-15/kg — best for small first orders under 100kg
  • Air Freight: 7-15 days, $3-8/kg — good for medium orders
  • Sea Freight (FCL/LCL): 25-40 days, $1-3/kg — cheapest for large orders but slow
  • For your first order, air express is likely the move — speed matters more than savings
  • Get the supplier to handle shipping (DDP = Delivered Duty Paid) if possible
  • If shipping yourself, use a freight forwarder (Flexport, Freightos, or find one on Alibaba)
Hair extensions are lightweight and high-value, making air shipping very cost-effective. A first order of 100 bundles might only weigh 10-15kg. At $10/kg via DHL, that's $100-150 in shipping — totally reasonable.
🏛️
Customs & Import Compliance
Clear your goods through U.S. customs without delays or surprises.
What You Need to Know
  • Hair extensions HS Code: typically 6703 (human hair, dressed/thinned) or 6704 (synthetic)
  • Import duty rate: varies by type, usually 0-5% for human hair
  • Orders under $800 may qualify for de minimis (no duty) — Section 321
  • You'll need: commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading/airway bill
  • If using DHL/FedEx, they handle customs clearance for you (built into the service)
  • For larger shipments, your freight forwarder handles the customs broker
For your first order, ship via DHL/FedEx and let them handle customs. It's slightly more expensive but eliminates the hassle of dealing with customs brokers yourself. As you scale, switch to sea freight with a freight forwarder.
📦
Receive, Inspect & Store Inventory
QC your bulk order upon arrival and set up your storage/fulfillment system.
Action Steps
  • Inspect EVERY unit when received — compare against your approved samples
  • Check: correct lengths, textures, colors, bond sizes, packaging, labeling
  • Test 5-10% of units with the same quality tests you ran on samples
  • Photograph any defects immediately and notify supplier within 48 hours
  • Set up storage: climate-controlled space, organized by SKU
  • Create an inventory tracking system (spreadsheet or Shopify inventory)
  • Consider a 3PL (third-party logistics) like ShipBob if you want hands-off fulfillment

05
Phase Five
Storefront, Content & Pre-Launch
Build your sales channels and create the content machine that will drive your launch. Start this while inventory is in transit.
Weeks 8-12
🛒
Build Your Shopify Store
Set up a professional e-commerce store that converts browsers into buyers.
Store Setup Checklist
  • Sign up for Shopify ($39/mo Basic plan is fine to start)
  • Buy a custom domain (yourbrand.com — $14/year through Shopify)
  • Choose a clean, premium theme (Dawn is free and great, or pay $180-350 for a premium theme)
  • Create product pages with professional photos, detailed specs, and care instructions
  • Write product descriptions that sell the experience, not just the specs
  • Set up payment processing (Shopify Payments, PayPal, Afterpay/Klarna for BNPL)
  • Add essential pages: About, Shipping Policy, Returns, FAQ, Contact
  • Set up email marketing (Klaviyo free tier) with welcome flow and abandoned cart
Buy Now Pay Later (Afterpay/Klarna) is huge for hair extensions. Your price point ($85-200+) is perfect for BNPL — it removes purchase friction and increases average order value by 20-30%.
📱
Social Media Setup
Build your Instagram, TikTok, and optional TikTok Shop presence.
Platform Setup
  • Instagram: Business account, branded bio, highlight covers, link in bio (Linktree or Stan Store)
  • TikTok: Business account, pin your best transformation videos
  • TikTok Shop: Apply for TikTok Shop seller account — huge for hair extensions right now
  • Create 15-20 pieces of content BEFORE launch day (bank content)
  • Content types: install videos, texture close-ups, before/after transformations, unboxings
  • Start posting 2-3 weeks before launch to build anticipation
TikTok Shop is the single biggest growth channel for hair extensions in 2025-2026. Stylists doing live installs with your product = direct sales. Set up affiliate commissions (15-20%) to incentivize creators to sell for you.
📸
Product Photography & Content
Shoot professional product and lifestyle content that sells.
Content to Create
  • Flat-lay product shots on clean background (for website product pages)
  • Model shots: hair installed, styled, in motion (for ads and social)
  • Close-up texture shots (buyers want to see the hair quality)
  • Unboxing videos showing your premium packaging
  • Before/after install transformations (your #1 selling content)
  • Reels/TikToks of the hair being washed, flat-ironed, curled (quality proof)
  • Hair care/maintenance tutorial content (adds value, builds trust)
You don't need a studio. Natural light near a window + an iPhone + a friend/model = 90% of what you need. Authenticity sells better than overproduced content in the hair space. Real-looking content converts higher.
🔥
Pre-Launch Hype
Build anticipation and collect emails/followers before you officially launch.
Pre-Launch Tactics
  • Create a "Coming Soon" landing page with email signup (offer 15% off for early subscribers)
  • Seed product to 3-5 micro-influencers or local stylists for reviews and install content
  • Tease on social: texture close-ups, packaging reveals, behind-the-scenes of the brand
  • Build a waitlist — creates urgency and gives you a launch-day email blast list
  • Set a public launch date and count down to it on social
Send free product to 3-5 stylists with 5K-50K followers — micro-influencers have higher engagement and conversion rates than big names. Offer them an affiliate code so they earn commission on every sale.

06
Phase Six
Launch, Fulfill & Scale
Go live, handle your first orders, gather feedback, and build the flywheel for sustainable growth.
Weeks 12-16+
🚀
Launch Day Execution
Coordinate your launch across all channels for maximum impact.
Launch Day Checklist
  • Blast your email waitlist with launch announcement + exclusive discount
  • Post across all social channels (IG, TikTok, stories, reels)
  • Have influencer posts go live on the same day (coordinate ahead)
  • Run a launch-day promotion: free shipping, bundle deal, or gift with purchase
  • Go live on IG or TikTok to show product and answer questions in real-time
  • Monitor everything: orders, website analytics, DMs, comments
  • Respond to every DM and comment within 1 hour on launch day
📬
Order Fulfillment
Ship orders fast with a premium unboxing experience.
Fulfillment Process
  • Ship within 1-2 business days (speed builds trust and reviews)
  • Use branded packaging: custom box → tissue → satin bag → product → care card → thank-you card
  • Include a care instruction card with every order
  • Include a discount code for their next purchase (drives repeat business)
  • Send shipping confirmation email with tracking number (Shopify does this automatically)
  • Follow up 7 days after delivery asking for a review (automate via Klaviyo)
  • Use USPS Priority or UPS for domestic (2-3 day delivery)
📊
Paid Ads & Growth
Scale beyond organic with targeted paid advertising.
Growth Channels
  • Meta Ads (IG/FB): Start with $20-50/day, retarget website visitors
  • TikTok Ads: Spark Ads (boost organic posts that perform well) — incredible for hair
  • Google Shopping: Catch people actively searching "buy butterfly weft extensions"
  • Influencer Partnerships: Scale from 5 to 20-50 affiliates over time
  • UGC (User Generated Content): Repost customer install photos/videos (with permission)
  • Track your cost per acquisition (CPA) — if it costs $20 to get a customer who spends $100+, that's great
Don't spend on ads until you have organic social proof (reviews, install videos, UGC). Ads amplify what's already working — they can't fix a product or brand that hasn't been validated organically first.
🔄
Reorder & Expand
Analyze what sold, restock winners, and plan your next product drop.
Scaling Decisions
  • Review sales data: which SKUs sold fastest? Which sat? Double down on winners.
  • Place reorder 2-3 weeks before you run out (account for production + shipping time)
  • Increase MOQ on reorders to get better per-unit pricing
  • Add new lengths, colors, or textures based on customer requests
  • Consider adding complementary products: edge control, silk pillowcases, hair tools
  • Build a subscription/auto-reorder option for repeat customers
  • Explore wholesale to salons once you've proven product-market fit
Hair extensions are a repeat-purchase product — customers come back every 3-6 months. Your email list is your most valuable asset. Focus on retention, not just acquisition. A customer who buys 4x is worth 4 new customers.
